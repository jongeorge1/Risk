Interpreting PSA Levels

Back

Interpretation of PSA levels vary according to your current treatment status. Click on an option below for more information:

During Active Monitoring (Surveillance)

Your PSA levels should be measured every 3 – 6 months. If your PSA level doubles within 2 years, you must contact your HCP.

After Surgical Removal of the Prostate (Prostatectomy)

If the PSA level rises to greater than 0.2ng/ml, you must contact your HCP.

After Radiotherapy or Brachytherapy Treatment

PSA slowly drops to its lowest level by 18 months (usually less than 1.0ng/ml).

If the PSA level then rises an additional 2.0ng/ml above this lowest level, you must contact your HCP.
